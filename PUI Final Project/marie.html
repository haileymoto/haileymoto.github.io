Marie’s Multiple Sclerosis (MS) sometimes makes it hard for her to remember the foods that relieve or worsen her symptoms. Drag food to Marie and see how different foods affect her.

Processed sugars, in foods like candy, are highly inflammatory and can increase susceptibility to MS-related fatigue.

Consumption of whole dairy in MS patients may have a possible role in progression or relapse of disease by triggering immune responses.

Vitamin D deficiency has been implicated as being a risk-factor in MS patients. Fatty fish, like tuna, is a great source of Vitamin D.

Refined carbohydrates like white bread can aggravate inflammation in the body, potentially making joints hurt more than they already do.

A study found MS patients with medium/high salt intake had clinical relapse rates 2.75- and 3.95-fold higher that patients with low salt intake.

Cruciferous vegetables, like broccoli provide the essential amino acid tryptophan. Tryptophan helps people better manage psychological stress.

What is Multiple Sclerosis?

Multiple Sclerosis (MS) is a disease in which the immune system mistakenly attacks the protective covering of nerve cells, which can lead to diminished function in the brain and spinal cord.

Common Symptoms

•Muscle weakness or spasms
•Numbness and tingling
•Dizziness, vertigo, and fatigue
•Brain fog and memory problems
