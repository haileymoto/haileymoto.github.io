Larry’s Lupus Erythematosus comes and goes in inflammatory “flares”, resulting in fatigue and a pretty bad rash. Drag food to Larry to see how different foods affect him.

Patients with lupus harbor fewer bacterial species in the gut. Yogurt contains probiotics, which helps to diversify gut bacteria.

Numerous animal studies have found that omega-3 fatty acids (abundant in walnuts) reduce severity of autoimmune diseases in mice.

Red meat and fried foods are high in saturated fat. Saturated fat stimulates the immune system, making it more active than it already is.

Though there is no scientific evidence, many lupus patients are sensitive to nightshade vegetables such as tomatoes and potatoes.

While garlic has many health benefits, lupus patients report garlic as a trigger for flares, possibly due to thiosulfinate allicin found in garlic.

Herbs such as ginger and turmeric are anti-oxidative and anti-inflammatory. They are helpful for patients suffering from arthritic symptoms.

What is Lupus Erythematosus?

Lupus is an autoimmune disease in which the immune system mistakenly attacks the body’s own tissues and organs. Inflammation caused by lupus can affect many different body systems such as joints, skin, kidneys, blood cells, brain, heart and lungs.

Common Symptoms

•Fatigue
•“Butterfly” facial rash
•Joint pain and stiffness
•Skin lesions as a result of sun exposure
